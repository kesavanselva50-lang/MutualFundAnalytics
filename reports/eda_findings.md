EDA Findings

1. Mutual fund NAVs showed a strong upward trend during 2023, indicating a broad market bull run across major schemes.

2. Several schemes experienced temporary corrections during 2024 before resuming growth.

3. SBI Mutual Fund maintained the highest AUM among fund houses, crossing approximately ₹12.5 lakh crore.

4. Monthly SIP inflows increased steadily from ₹11,517 crore in January 2022 to ₹31,002 crore in December 2025.

5. Retail participation increased significantly as total folio counts grew from 13.26 crore to 26.12 crore.

6. Liquid Funds consistently attracted the highest net inflows among mutual fund categories.

7. Flexi Cap, Mid Cap, and Small Cap categories showed strong investor preference and sustained inflows.

8. Most mutual fund schemes exhibited positive return correlations, suggesting common exposure to broader market movements.

9. Banking and Financial sectors accounted for a significant share of portfolio allocations across equity funds.

10. Investors aged 26–45 represented a major portion of mutual fund participation, while T30 cities continued to contribute a substantial share of investments.
